Publications

Opioid overdoses increase at home during the COVID-19 stay-at-home order period in Cook County, Illinois
AJPM Focus June 15, 2022
Dr. Daniel Harris Dr. Chris Delcher Nick Anthony

Stay-at-home orders during the COVID-19 pandemic decreased population mobility to reduce SARS-CoV-2 infection rates. We empirically tested the hypothesis that this public health measure was associated with a higher likelihood of opioid- and stimulant-involved deaths occurring in homes located in Cook County, Illinois.

Presented at AMIA November 5, 2022
Dr. Daniel Harris Dr. Chris Delcher Nick Anthony

Provides a framework for conducting geospatial analysis on Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) within a targeted spatial region. Built an end-to-end data pipeline that consumes data from multiple sources, performs geocoding and spatial joining, extracts drugs mentions, automates descriptive analysis and reporting, and publishes the enhanced data for consumption by internal and external researchers.

Social Determinants of Health from Electronic Medical Records of Patients with Substance Use Disorders during the “Triple Wave”
Pharmacology Biochemistry and Behavior Released soon...
Dr. Daniel Harris Dr. Chris Delcher Nick Anthony

This study uses electronic medical records (EMRs) from a safety net hospital system from 14,032 patients in Kentucky from 2017 to 2019 in order to 1) define three types of SUD cohorts with shared/unique risk factors, 2) identify patients with unstable housing using novel methods for EMRs and 3) link patients to their residential neighborhood to obtain quantitative perspective on social vulnerability.
